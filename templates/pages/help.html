Help

Voting

Voting is one of the fundamentals of LUKSense. There is no need for human moderators to check the activities. Nor are any specific rules. Everybody whose prestige is greater than zero can vote on NFTs. Votes are the rules and the moderators of the community and that's why sincere voting is of vital importance.

In fact, voting authenticity has been thoroughly taken into consideration. It is a primary component of the system's backend algorithm. To protect the community from malicious parties, destructive voting patterns are detected and proper action is taken. The logic behind it, is quite simple: "If your opinion is always different, you don't belong to the community." By the way, you don't have to worry if you don't have malicious intentions. Obviously, it's all right to have a different opinion occasionally.

Prestige

As the name suggests, prestige indicates how favourably you're regarded in the community. Newcomers start with prestige=0 and based on their activity, it may go up or down. Prestige is assigned based on a rather complicated calculation. There are several activities that can affect your prestige including but not limited to:

Positive:

  1. Getting good votes on your NFTs.
  2. Sincere voting.

Negative:

  1. Getting bad votes on your NFTs.
  2. Not voting on most of your purchased NFTs.
  3. Cluster voting e.g. creating multiple accounts to vote up each other.
  4. Repeated outlier voting e.g. trying to damage the prestige of others by bad votes.
  5. Selfish voting e.g. refusing to vote on good NFTs, but voting on bad NFTs.

Basically, higher prestige means higher privileges. There are certain actions that require a minimum prestige. It's also worth noting that prestige value grows logarithmically, therefore getting prestige=6 isn't three times harder than getting prestige=2 (it's way much harder).
